Mission

The Department of Chemistry is dedicated to advancing fundamental and applied chemical knowledge through rigorous research, innovative teaching, and collaborative discovery. We prepare students to become leaders in academia, industry, and public service, fostering a culture of intellectual curiosity and scientific integrity that serves both the university community and the broader world.

Vision

To be a global leader in chemical research and education, driving transformative discoveries that address the most urgent challenges in health, energy, materials, and the environment. We envision a department where interdisciplinary collaboration, diversity of thought, and a commitment to excellence converge to shape the future of molecular science.

Research Facilities

State-of-the-art laboratories and instrumentation supporting groundbreaking chemical research.

NMR Spectroscopy Facility

Solid-state and solution-state NMR instruments ranging from 300 MHz to 800 MHz, including cryoprobe and variable temperature capabilities.

Mass Spectrometry Laboratory

UHPLC-QTOF, Orbitrap-LC-MS, GC-QQQ-MS, and GC-MS systems for high-resolution molecular analysis and proteomics.

Analytical Core Facilities

Comprehensive instrumentation including UV/Vis, ATR-FTIR, Raman, fluorescence spectroscopy, and X-ray fluorescence for materials characterization.

NMR Spectroscopy Facility

Advanced Magnetic Resonance for Structural Elucidation

The NMR facility supports research across organic chemistry, biochemistry, materials science, and metabolomics. Instruments are managed by dedicated scientific staff and available to all department members.

  • Solid-State NMR: 300 MHz, 400 MHz, 500 MHz, 700 MHz with variable temperature and magic-angle spinning
  • Solution-State NMR: 400 MHz, 500 MHz, 700 MHz, 800 MHz with cryoprobes and autosamplers
  • Diffusion NMR: Capability for DOSY and diffusion-ordered spectroscopy
  • EPR Spectrometers: 4 units for electron paramagnetic resonance studies
  • Sample Preparation: Automated pipetting robots, vortex mixers, and temperature-controlled storage
  • Data Processing: High-performance workstations with advanced spectral analysis software
Open Access Training Available

Mass Spectrometry Laboratory

High-Resolution Molecular Analysis and Proteomics

The mass spectrometry facility provides state-of-the-art instrumentation for the identification, quantification, and structural characterization of small molecules, biomolecules, and complex mixtures.

  • UHPLC-QTOF: 4 units for high-resolution accurate mass measurements
  • Orbitrap-LC-MS: 4 units for high-sensitivity proteomics and metabolomics
  • GC-QQQ-MS: 3 units for targeted quantitative analysis of volatile compounds
  • GC-MS: 2 units for routine mass spectral analysis
  • Ion Chromatographs: 4 units with IC-MS-ESI systems
  • Analytical HPLC: 10 units with GPC/SEC capabilities
  • Data Systems: Integrated spectral libraries and compound identification databases
User Training Required Collaborative Research

Analytical Core Facilities

Comprehensive Materials Characterization

The Analytical Core Facilities provide a broad range of spectroscopic and analytical capabilities for the characterization of chemical compounds, materials, and biological samples.

  • UV/Vis Spectroscopy: Multiple instruments for routine and advanced absorbance measurements
  • UV/Vis-NIR: Extended wavelength range for materials characterization
  • ATR-FTIR: Fourier-transform infrared spectroscopy with attenuated total reflectance
  • Fluorescence Spectrometer: Steady-state and time-resolved fluorescence measurements
  • Raman CLSM: Confocal Raman microscopy for spatial chemical mapping
  • XRF Spectrometer: Elemental analysis of solid and liquid samples
  • Circular Dichroism Spectrometer: For chiral molecule and protein conformational analysis
  • Particle Size Analyzer: Dynamic light scattering for nanoparticle characterization
Core Facility Fee-for-Service

Synthetic Chemistry Laboratories

Advanced Synthesis and Reaction Engineering

The synthetic chemistry facilities support research in organic, inorganic, and organometallic synthesis, with a focus on the development of new reactions, catalysts, and functional materials.

  • Fume Hoods: Over 50 individual hoods for safe chemical handling
  • Schlenk Lines: Air-free synthesis for oxygen- and moisture-sensitive compounds
  • Gloveboxes: Multiple units for inert-atmosphere synthesis
  • Rotary Evaporators: 20 units with vacuum pumps for solvent removal
  • High-Pressure Reactors: For high-pressure reactions and catalytic hydrogenation
  • Parallel Synthesizers: Automated synthesis platforms for combinatorial chemistry
  • Reaction Calorimeter: For reaction thermochemistry and process safety studies
  • Vacuum Pumps: High-vacuum systems for distillation and sublimation
24/7 Access Safety Training Required

X-Ray Diffraction Facility

Structural Determination of Molecular and Crystalline Materials

The XRD facility enables the determination of molecular and crystal structures essential for understanding the relationship between structure and function in chemical and materials research.

  • Single-Crystal X-Ray Diffractometers: 4 systems with variable temperature capability
  • Powder X-Ray Diffractometers: 4 systems for phase identification and quantitative analysis
  • High-Resolution XRF: Elemental analysis with micro-focus capability
  • X-Ray MicroCT: 3D imaging for non-destructive materials characterization
  • XPS/UPS: X-ray and ultraviolet photoelectron spectroscopy for surface analysis
  • SAXS/WAXS: Small- and wide-angle X-ray scattering for nanostructure studies
  • Data Processing: Advanced software for structure solution and refinement
Crystallography Training Collaborative Access

Electrochemistry Laboratory

Energy Storage and Electrocatalysis Research

The electrochemistry facility supports research in batteries, fuel cells, electrolysis, and electrocatalysis, with a focus on sustainable energy conversion and storage technologies.

  • Potentiostats/Galvanostats: Multiple units for electrochemical measurements
  • Battery Cyclers: High-precision cycling stations for battery testing
  • Electrochemical Impedance Spectroscopy: Systems for frequency response analysis
  • Rotating Disk Electrode Systems: For hydrodynamic voltammetry studies
  • Conductivity Meters: For solution and solid-state conductivity measurements
  • Scanning Electrochemical Microscope: For local electrochemical surface characterization
  • Glovebox Integration: Air-free assembly and testing of battery materials

Research Areas

Our faculty pursue transformative research across the breadth of the chemical sciences.

Organic & Synthetic Chemistry

Development of new synthetic methodologies, total synthesis of natural products, and the design of functional molecules for pharmaceuticals and materials.

Inorganic & Organometallic Chemistry

Synthesis of novel coordination compounds, catalysis, bioinorganic chemistry, and the development of metal-based therapeutics and materials.

Physical & Theoretical Chemistry

Quantum chemical calculations, molecular dynamics, reaction dynamics, and the development of theoretical models for complex chemical systems.

Analytical Chemistry

Advanced separation techniques, mass spectrometry, spectroscopy, and the development of new analytical methods for chemical detection and quantification.

Biochemistry & Chemical Biology

Protein structure and function, enzyme catalysis, drug discovery, and the application of chemical principles to biological problems.

Materials Chemistry

Design and synthesis of novel materials for energy storage, catalysis, electronic devices, and environmental remediation.

Computational Chemistry

Development and application of computational methods for molecular modeling, drug design, and the prediction of chemical properties and reactivity.

Environmental Chemistry

Study of chemical processes in the environment, pollution remediation, green chemistry, and the development of sustainable chemical technologies.
