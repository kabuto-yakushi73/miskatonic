Distinctions

Gnostic Seminary

The only continuously operating Gnostic seminary within an American research university, rooted in the scholarly traditions of the Hermetic Order.

Rare Manuscripts

Home to the Miskatonic Collection of Non-Canonical and Esoteric Literature, including the Codex Leningradensis and unique biblical fragments.

Ecumenical & Comparative

Faculty expertise spans Orthodox, Catholic, Protestant, Jewish, Islamic, Buddhist, and Zoroastrian traditions, with a focus on comparative doctrine.

Pastoral Formation

Formation for ordained ministry in Gnostic, Anglican, and ecumenical contexts, supported by the Miskatonic University Church and local partnerships.

Mission & Vision

Mission

The Miskatonic Divinity School cultivates rigorous theological inquiry, pastoral formation, and interreligious understanding. We prepare scholars and ministers who can engage the deepest questions of faith, history, and human existence—integrating the intellectual traditions of the university with the spiritual practices of the Church.

— Angus Smith, Ph.D., Dean

Vision

To be a global center for the study of theology, eschatology, and the formation of religious life, where the ancient wisdom of the Gnostic tradition meets the critical methods of modern scholarship—serving the Church and the academy with integrity, humility, and intellectual courage.

Research Centers & Initiatives

Codex Leningradensis Research Lab

Textual analysis, paleography, and digital preservation of the Leningrad Codex and related Hebrew Bible manuscripts.

Doctrinal Boundary Research Initiative

Examination of heterodox and esoteric texts, exploring the historical development of religious boundaries and mystical traditions.

FISH Lab

Formation and Intersections of Sacred Histories — comparative studies of ancient Near Eastern, biblical, and patristic sources.

Miskatonic Apologetics Lab

Philosophical and theological defense of Christian faith in conversation with modern science, secularism, and other worldviews.

Metaphysical Studies at Miskatonic

Interdisciplinary research in ontology, philosophy of religion, and mystical theology, with a focus on modal metaphysics and divine attributes.

Comparative Esoteric Traditions

Global study of esoteric, mystical, and occult movements across Christian, Jewish, Islamic, and Indic traditions.

Ministry & Community Engagement

Pastoral Formation

Clinical training, spiritual direction, and field placements in parishes, hospitals, and social service agencies throughout New England.

Miskatonic University Church

The spiritual heart of the campus, offering daily offices, weekly worship, and seasonal festivals open to the university community.

Ecumenical & Interfaith Outreach

Partnerships with local congregations, Catholic Charities, and interfaith organizations serving Arkham and the surrounding region.

Alumni in Ministry

Graduates serve in parishes, chaplaincies, and academic posts across the United States and internationally, carrying the Miskatonic tradition of theological excellence and pastoral care.
