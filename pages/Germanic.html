Welcome

The Department of Germanic and Nordic Studies at Miskatonic University is a vibrant center for the study of the languages, literatures, and cultures of Northern Europe. From Old Norse sagas to contemporary Scandinavian cinema, from the philology of Gothic to the poetry of Rilke, our faculty and students explore the full breadth of Germanic and Nordic heritage.

With 19 regular faculty, 119 students, and a curriculum that spans medieval to modern, the department is one of the most comprehensive programs of its kind in North America. Our research strengths include Germanic philology, Old Norse literature, Finno-Ugric studies, and the cultural history of the Nordic world.

About the Department

History • Mission • Legacy

Our Story

Founded in 1892 as the Department of Germanic Philology, our department has grown to encompass the full range of Germanic and Nordic languages and cultures. We are home to one of the oldest continuously operating programs in Germanic linguistics in the United States.

Mission

To advance the study of Germanic and Nordic languages, literatures, and cultures through rigorous scholarship, innovative teaching, and a commitment to preserving and interpreting the textual and oral traditions of Northern Europe.

Vision

To be the leading center for Germanic and Nordic studies in the Americas, recognized for our expertise in historical linguistics, medieval literature, and the cultural heritage of the Nordic and Germanic-speaking worlds.

Areas of Strength: Germanic Philology • Old Norse & Medieval Scandinavian • Finno-Ugric Studies • German Literature from the Middle Ages to the Present • Nordic Cultural Studies • Yiddish and Ashkenazi Cultural History • Dutch and Low Countries Studies

📚 Special Collections & Archives

Germanic Philology Library: Over 50,000 volumes, including rare editions of medieval German, Old Norse, and Yiddish texts.

Nordic Cultural Archive: Manuscripts, sound recordings, and ephemera documenting Nordic folk traditions.

Finno-Ugric Resource Center: A specialized collection of materials on the languages and cultures of the Finno-Ugric peoples.
