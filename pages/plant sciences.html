Welcome to Plant Sciences

Advancing knowledge in plant biology, agriculture, and sustainable ecosystems.

The Department of Plant Sciences at Wadsworth Estate is a world‑renowned centre for research and education in plant biology, crop science, and environmental sustainability. Our mission is to understand the fundamental processes of plant life and apply that knowledge to secure food systems, restore ecosystems, and combat climate change.

With state‑of‑the‑art laboratories, glasshouses, and extensive field stations, we offer a unique environment for students and researchers to explore the future of plant science.

About the Department

History, mission, and impact of Plant Sciences at Wadsworth.

Founded: 1913 with the donation of the Wadsworth Estate.

Mission: To advance the understanding of plant systems through innovative research, rigorous education, and meaningful partnerships with agricultural communities and industries.

Vision: To be a global leader in plant sciences, addressing the challenges of food security, climate resilience, and sustainable land use.

The department is housed within the historic Cabot Building and operates over 200 hectares of experimental plantations, greenhouses, and livestock research facilities. Our interdisciplinary teams work on everything from molecular genetics to ecosystem ecology.
