Mission

The Department of Russian, Balkan, and Eastern European Studies is committed to advancing the study of the languages, literatures, histories, and societies of a diverse and geopolitically vital region. Through rigorous philological training, historical inquiry, and interdisciplinary research, we prepare students to become global citizens, scholars, and professionals who understand the complexities of Eastern Europe and Eurasia.

Vision

To be a preeminent center for Eastern European and Eurasian studies in North America, fostering innovative research that bridges linguistic, cultural, and political divides. We envision a department that serves as a hub for scholarly exchange, public engagement, and policy-relevant knowledge, contributing to a deeper understanding of a region that continues to shape the course of global history.

Slavic Language Media Center

Immersive Language Acquisition

The Slavic Language Media Center provides students with the tools to achieve proficiency in the languages of Eastern Europe through immersive, technology-enhanced learning.

  • Satellite TV & Radio: Live broadcasts from Russia, Poland, Ukraine, Serbia, and Bulgaria
  • Audio-Visual Recording Suites: 8 individual booths for pronunciation practice and recording
  • Computer Workstations: 30 stations with language learning software, corpora, and digital dictionaries
  • Media Collection: Over 5,000 films, documentaries, and audio recordings in regional languages
  • Conversation Groups: Dedicated spaces for informal language practice with native speakers
  • Faculty Support: Language coordinators available for individualized instruction and tutoring
  • Virtual Exchange: High-definition video conferencing for direct connections with partner universities abroad
Open to All Students Reservation Required

Balkan Folklore Archive

Preserving Oral and Cultural Heritage

The Balkan Folklore Archive is one of the most comprehensive repositories of Balkan folk culture outside the region, containing materials collected over seven decades.

  • Field Recordings: Over 3,000 hours of audio recordings of folk songs, epic poetry, and oral narratives
  • Manuscripts: Hundreds of transcribed texts, including guslar poems, riddles, and proverbs
  • Ethnographic Documents: Photographs, drawings, and descriptions of traditional customs, rituals, and costumes
  • Musical Instruments: A collection of traditional instruments used in educational demonstrations
  • Scholarly Database: Fully searchable digital index of all materials, accessible to researchers worldwide
  • Research Fellowships: Opportunities for visiting scholars to work with the collection
  • Public Programming: Regular lectures, concerts, and workshops showcasing Balkan cultural traditions
By Appointment Digital Access Available

Eastern European Geopolitical Library

Policy-Ready Research Resources

The Geopolitical Library offers a specialized collection that supports research on the political, economic, and security dynamics of Eastern Europe and Eurasia.

  • Monographs: Over 8,000 volumes on Eastern European history, political science, and international relations
  • Periodicals: Current and historical subscriptions to leading regional journals and newspapers
  • Government Documents: Official publications from the Russian Federation, EU, NATO, and OSCE
  • Archival Collections: Microfilmed and digitized diplomatic records from the 19th and 20th centuries
  • Statistical Databases: Economic and demographic data for all countries in the region
  • Current Awareness: Daily news digests and policy briefings provided to faculty and students
  • Collaborative Space: Seminar rooms for policy workshops and research presentations
Open to Faculty & Graduate Students Reference Assistance

Uralic-Altaic Research Collection

Linguistic Treasures of Eurasia

This specialized collection is a vital resource for the study of the Uralic and Altaic language families, supporting research in historical linguistics, philology, and cultural studies.

  • Grammars & Dictionaries: Over 2,000 volumes covering Finnish, Estonian, Hungarian, Sami, Turkish, Mongolian, Manchu, and more
  • Corpus Linguistics: Digital and printed corpora for comparative and historical linguistic analysis
  • Ethnographic Works: Studies on the traditional cultures and religions of Uralic and Altaic peoples
  • Rare Books: 19th-century missionary grammars and early linguistic descriptions
  • Language Revitalization: Materials documenting efforts to preserve endangered languages of Siberia and the Urals
  • Research Support: Faculty experts in Uralic and Altaic linguistics available for consultation
  • Collaborative Projects: Active partnerships with institutions in Finland, Hungary, Russia, and Mongolia
Specialized Access By Permission

Russian and Eastern European Archive Research Center

Primary Sources for Historical and Cultural Research

The Russian and Eastern European Archive Research Center provides scholars with access to primary sources documenting the political, cultural, social, and intellectual history of Russia, the Balkans, and Eastern Europe from the medieval period to the contemporary era.

  • Historical Documents: Collections of manuscripts, diplomatic correspondence, political documents, and administrative records
  • Rare Book Collection: Early printed works, historical monographs, and regional publications in Russian and other Eastern European languages
  • Digitization Suite: High-resolution scanning equipment for preservation and digital scholarship projects
  • Oral History Repository: Recorded interviews documenting migration, cultural memory, and regional experiences
  • Genealogy and Historical Records: Resources for demographic, family history, and population studies
  • Research Workstations: Dedicated archival research stations with access to international databases and digital collections
  • Visiting Scholar Program: Research space and support services for scholars working on Eastern European history and culture

Research Areas

Our faculty and students pursue innovative research across the languages, literatures, histories, and politics of Eastern Europe and Eurasia.

Slavic & Balkan Linguistics

The study of Slavic and Balkan languages, including phonology, morphology, syntax, and sociolinguistics, with a focus on language contact and historical development.

Eastern European Literature & Culture

The literary traditions of Russia, Ukraine, Poland, and the Balkans, from medieval chronicles to contemporary postcolonial and post-Soviet writing.

Eastern European History & Memory

The political, social, and cultural history of Eastern Europe, with special attention to imperial and post-imperial legacies, nationalism, and collective memory.

Uralic & Altaic Philology

The study of Uralic and Altaic language families, including linguistic reconstruction, comparative grammar, and the documentation of endangered languages.

Balkan Folklore & Ethnography

The oral traditions, rituals, and material culture of the Balkan peoples, with a focus on epic poetry, folk music, and traditional customs.

Central Asian & Eurasian Studies

The histories, cultures, and political systems of Central Asia, the Caucasus, and the broader Eurasian space, with an emphasis on post-Soviet transitions.

Eastern European Politics & International Relations

The domestic politics, foreign policies, and security dynamics of Eastern European states, including EU and NATO integration, and relations with Russia.

Religious & Intellectual History

The religious traditions, philosophical movements, and intellectual history of Eastern Europe and the Balkans, from Orthodoxy to dissident thought.
