A Legacy of Discovery

The Physics Department at Miskatonic University traces its roots to the early 19th century, when natural philosophy gave way to experimental physics. Today, we are a global leader in theoretical and applied physics, with pioneering work in quantum information, condensed matter, cosmology, and high-energy particle physics.

Our faculty includes recipients of the Nobel Prize, the Dirac Medal, and the Breakthrough Prize. We are home to the Warren Observatory and maintain close partnerships with the Arkham National Laboratory and the Miskatonic Light Source — a 5 GeV synchrotron that enables groundbreaking experiments in materials science and quantum phenomena.

Research Excellence

Our research spans the smallest scales of quantum fields to the largest structures in the cosmos.

Quantum Gravity & Strings

Exploring Calabi–Yau manifolds, M-theory, and the quantum structure of spacetime. Our group works on compactification, duality, and emergent gravity.

High-Energy Astrophysics

From gamma-ray bursts to active galactic nuclei, we study the most energetic phenomena in the universe using space-based observatories and the Warren Observatory.

Condensed Matter & Quantum Materials

Topological insulators, high-temperature superconductors, and 2D materials — investigated with advanced spectroscopy and nanofabrication at the ANL.

Cosmology & Dark Energy

Large-scale structure surveys, cosmic microwave background analysis, and theoretical modeling of dark energy and modified gravity.

Quantum Information Science

Quantum computing, entanglement, and quantum metrology. Our group collaborates with the ANL–IBM Quantum Computing Facility to develop next-generation quantum processors.

Particle Physics & Accelerator Science

We are partners in the LHC, LIGO, and the Fermilab neutrino program. Our faculty contribute to the search for physics beyond the Standard Model.

World-Class Facilities

From the Arkham Hills to the subglacial labs of Antarctica — our infrastructure is built for discovery.

Warren Observatory

150‑inch & 100‑inch telescopes · radio array · exoplanet detection · located in the Arkham Hills.

Miskatonic Light Source

5 GeV synchrotron with 23 beamlines · X‑ray spectroscopy · materials science · located at ANL.

ANL–IBM Quantum Computing

Trapped-ion & neutral-atom quantum processors · 800‑node Orion Cluster · 40 PB data center.

Pabodie Research Station

Antarctica · ice‑core drilling · neutrino detection · co‑managed with NOAA and Miskatonic University.

Sebastian Duvall Hall

Main physics building · 20 teaching labs · 20 computer labs · research electronics shop · modern expressionist architecture.

Ultracold & Cryogenics Lab

Dilution refrigerators · vacuum & atom trapping · metastable helium spectroscopy · < 10 mK base temperature.
