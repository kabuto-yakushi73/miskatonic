Mission

The Department of South and Southeast Asian Studies is dedicated to advancing the understanding of the languages, literatures, religions, histories, and contemporary societies of South and Southeast Asia. Through rigorous philological training, interdisciplinary research, and immersive field experience, we prepare students to become scholars, educators, and professionals who engage meaningfully with this dynamic and globally significant region.

Vision

To be a leading center for South and Southeast Asian studies in North America, fostering innovative research that bridges linguistic, cultural, and disciplinary boundaries. We envision a department that serves as a hub for scholarly exchange, public engagement, and policy-relevant knowledge, contributing to a deeper, more nuanced understanding of a region that is central to the global future.

South Asian Manuscript Library

Preserving the Literary Heritage of South Asia

The South Asian Manuscript Library holds one of the most significant collections of South Asian manuscripts in North America, supporting research in Indology, religious studies, and historical linguistics.

  • Collection Size: Over 5,000 manuscripts in Sanskrit, Tamil, Bengali, Urdu, and Prakrit
  • Material Types: Palm-leaf, paper, and birch-bark codices from the 12th to 19th centuries
  • Subject Areas: Vedic literature, Puranic texts, philosophy, grammar, poetry, and scientific treatises
  • Digital Access: High-resolution digitization of select manuscripts available through the university portal
  • Reading Room: A dedicated space for researchers with reference materials and finding aids
  • Conservation: A specialized conservation laboratory for the preservation of fragile materials
  • Research Fellowships: Visiting scholar programs for manuscript-based research
By Appointment Digital Access Available

Southeast Asian Cultural Archive

Documenting the Living Traditions of Southeast Asia

The Southeast Asian Cultural Archive preserves and provides access to a rich collection of ethnographic materials documenting the diverse cultures of the region.

  • Ethnographic Collection: Over 8,000 photographs, field notes, and ethnographic descriptions
  • Audiovisual Archive: 2,500+ hours of audio and video recordings of rituals, music, and oral traditions
  • Geographic Coverage: Thailand, Vietnam, Indonesia, Philippines, Malaysia, Cambodia, and Laos
  • Subject Areas: Traditional arts, religion, ritual, material culture, and social organization
  • Digital Database: Fully searchable catalog with high-resolution images and transcriptions
  • Teaching Resources: Curated sets of materials for classroom use and public exhibitions
  • Collaborative Projects: Partnerships with museums and cultural institutions in Southeast Asia
Open to Researchers By Appointment

Sanskrit & Prakrit Research Center

The Study of Classical Indian Languages

The Sanskrit & Prakrit Research Center provides an intellectual home for the study of classical Indian languages, supporting both teaching and research in these foundational traditions.

  • Reference Library: Over 3,000 volumes in Sanskrit and Prakrit studies, including critical editions and commentaries
  • Digital Corpora: Searchable databases of Sanskrit and Prakrit texts for linguistic and literary analysis
  • Reading Room: A quiet space for research with access to lexicons, grammars, and reference works
  • Teaching Collection: Curated materials for language instruction at all levels
  • Research Support: Faculty experts available for consultation and mentorship
  • Workshops: Regular workshops on paleography, manuscript reading, and textual criticism
  • Collaborative Initiatives: Partnerships with institutions in India, Nepal, and Sri Lanka
Open to All Workshops Available

Digital Southeast Asia Lab

Computational Approaches to Regional Studies

The Digital Southeast Asia Lab supports innovative research using computational methods to study the languages, literatures, and history of Southeast Asia.

  • Text Analysis Workstations: High-performance computers for natural language processing and corpus linguistics
  • Digital Mapping: GIS stations for historical and cultural mapping of Southeast Asia
  • Data Visualization: Large-format screens for presenting complex data
  • Digitization Equipment: Scanners and cameras for creating digital surrogates of archival materials
  • Software Suite: Access to specialized tools for text mining, network analysis, and digital publishing
  • Project Support: Technical assistance for designing and executing digital humanities projects
  • Collaborative Workspace: A flexible area for team-based research and interdisciplinary projects
Open to All Training Provided

Sanskrit Epigraphy Project

Digital Edition of South Asian Inscriptions

This multi-year project aims to create a comprehensive digital corpus of Sanskrit inscriptions from South and Southeast Asia, making these vital historical sources accessible to researchers worldwide.

  • Geographic Scope: India, Nepal, Sri Lanka, Indonesia, Cambodia, and Thailand
  • Chronological Range: 5th to 18th centuries CE
  • Materials: Stone, copper-plate, and palm-leaf inscriptions
  • Digital Edition: Critical editions with translation, commentary, and metadata
  • Public Access: Open-access database with search and visualization tools

Involved Faculty:

  • Prof. David Robert Galvasanker
  • Prof. Vilma Jewels
  • Dr. Anand Venkatesh
Ongoing Open Access

Oral Traditions of Southeast Asia

Documenting Epic Poetry and Folk Narratives

This project documents and analyzes the rich oral traditions of Southeast Asia, focusing on epic poetry, folk narratives, and ritual speech that are central to the region's cultural heritage.

  • Field Sites: Thailand (Isan region), Indonesia (Java and Bali), and the Philippines (Luzon and Mindanao)
  • Genres: Epic poetry, folk tales, myths, and ritual chants
  • Documentation: Audio and video recordings, transcription, translation, and analysis
  • Community Engagement: Collaboration with local communities and cultural organizations
  • Publications: Scholarly editions, monographs, and public-facing digital resources

Involved Faculty:

  • Prof. Amir Hakim
  • Prof. Phuwanai Intaraksa
  • Prof. Luqman Farid
  • Dr. Dewi Ratnasari
Ongoing Collaborative

Ayurveda & Traditional Medicine

Textual Traditions and Contemporary Practice

This project examines the history and textual traditions of Ayurvedic and Unani medicine in South Asia, exploring the intersection of classical knowledge and contemporary practice.

  • Textual Focus: Sanskrit and Arabic medical treatises, commentaries, and pharmacopoeias
  • Historical Context: The development of medical knowledge in South Asia from antiquity to the colonial period
  • Contemporary Practice: Ethnographic study of traditional medicine practitioners in India and Pakistan
  • Interdisciplinary Approach: Combining philology, history, anthropology, and medical sciences
  • Digital Humanities: Creation of searchable corpora of medical texts and databases of medicinal plants

Involved Faculty:

  • Prof. Sundar Rajan
  • Prof. David Robert Galvasanker
  • Dr. Priya Kapoor
  • Dr. Ravi Shankar Mehta
Ongoing Interdisciplinary

Comparative Indology

Intellectual Exchanges Across South and Southeast Asia

This project explores the intellectual and cultural exchanges between South and Southeast Asia, examining the transmission of philosophical, linguistic, and literary traditions across the region.

  • Thematic Focus: Sanskrit literary traditions, Buddhist philosophy, and the spread of Indic scripts
  • Geographic Scope: India, Sri Lanka, Indonesia, Cambodia, Thailand, and Vietnam
  • Comparative Methods: Philological analysis, textual criticism, and historical linguistics
  • Collaborative Network: Partnerships with universities in South and Southeast Asia
  • Publications: Critical editions, monographs, and conference proceedings

Involved Faculty:

  • Prof. Vilma Jewels
  • Prof. Kamala Subramanian
  • Prof. Anika Bose
  • Dr. Ritika Mehra
