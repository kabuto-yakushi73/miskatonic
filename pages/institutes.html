Medieval Institute

Chamberlain Castle · Est. 1671

The Medieval Institute is a premier center for the study of the Middle Ages, housed in the historic Chamberlain Castle. The institute brings together scholars from history, literature, art history, philosophy, and theology to explore the rich complexity of medieval civilization.

Key Research Areas:

  • Medieval History & Society
  • Medieval Literature & Philology
  • Medieval Art & Architecture
  • Manuscript Studies & Codicology
  • Medieval Philosophy & Theology

Facilities: Chamberlain Castle, Albert Richmond Medieval Library, Castle Armory, Conservation Laboratory, and Medieval Garden.

Dean: Rosalind Stark, Ph.D.

Institute for Data Science

MIDaS · Advancing Computational Discovery

The Institute for Data Science (MIDaS) is a hub for research and education in machine learning, data analytics, and computational modeling. Our faculty and students work across disciplines to develop innovative solutions to challenges in healthcare, climate science, social policy, and beyond.

Key Research Areas:

  • Machine Learning & AI
  • Data Visualization & Communication
  • Predictive Analytics & Forecasting
  • Data Ethics & Governance
  • Big Data Infrastructure

Facilities: High-performance computing clusters, data visualization labs, and collaborative workspaces.

Director: Oscar G. Ferreira, Ph.D.

Salem Institute

Historical & Cultural Heritage

The Salem Institute is dedicated to the preservation and study of New England's historical and cultural heritage. With deep roots in the region's esoteric and folk traditions, the institute maintains exceptional archival collections and supports research in history, anthropology, and material culture.

Key Research Areas:

  • New England History & Folklore
  • Material Culture & Artifact Studies
  • Esoteric & Folk Traditions
  • Cultural Heritage Preservation
  • Historical Archaeology

Facilities: Salem Hall, Special Collections Library, Conservation Lab, and public exhibition spaces.

Director: Armando Oliveira, Ph.D.

Sterling Brain Institute

Neuroscience · Cognition · Discovery

The Sterling Brain Institute (SBI) is a global leader in neuroscience research, exploring the structure and function of the brain at every level — from molecules to behavior. Our work advances understanding of neurological diseases, cognitive processes, and the fundamental nature of consciousness.

Key Research Areas:

  • Molecular & Cellular Neuroscience
  • Cognitive Neuroscience & Memory
  • Neurodegenerative Disease
  • Neuropharmacology & Toxicology
  • Brain Imaging & Neurotechnology

Facilities: MRI and EEG imaging suites, Miskatonic Mnemosyne Vault, and state-of-the-art research laboratories.

Director: Gerda Fallon, Ph.D.
