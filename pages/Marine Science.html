Welcome

The Miskatonic University Department of Marine Sciences is a world-leading center for oceanographic research, marine biology, and coastal ecology. Operating from the historic Somerset Manor in Innsmouth, our faculty and students explore the vast, mysterious depths of the world's oceans — from the sunlit shallows to the abyssal plains.

Our mission is to advance the understanding of marine ecosystems, train the next generation of ocean scientists, and serve as stewards of the marine environment. With state-of-the-art laboratories, a dedicated research vessel, and close ties to the Arkham National Laboratory, we are uniquely positioned to address the most pressing questions in marine science.

About the Department

History • Mission • Legacy

Our Story

The Marine Sciences Department traces its roots to the Somerset family, who donated their 93-acre coastal estate to Miskatonic University in 1951. The Somerset Manor, with its sweeping views of the Atlantic, has since grown into a premier marine research facility.

Mission

To advance the understanding of marine systems through interdisciplinary research, to educate the next generation of ocean leaders, and to provide scientific guidance for the conservation and sustainable use of marine resources.

Vision

To be the preeminent center for marine research in the Northeast, recognized globally for our contributions to ocean science, deep-sea exploration, and the study of marine biodiversity in a changing climate.

Innsmouth Research Station — Our primary field facility, located 150 km from the Miskatonic Main Campus, provides direct access to the North Atlantic's diverse marine environments. The station includes a research pier, submersible hangar, hydroacoustic listening station, and the historic Somerset Chateaux.
