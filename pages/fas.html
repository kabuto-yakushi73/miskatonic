About the Faculty of Arts & Sciences

The largest academic unit at Miskatonic University, the Faculty of Arts and Sciences is the intellectual heart of the university, encompassing 39 departments and programs across four divisions.

Our Mission

The Faculty of Arts and Sciences advances knowledge across the humanities, social sciences, and natural sciences. We cultivate critical thinking, creativity, and a deep understanding of the human condition and the natural world.

By the Numbers

  • 39 Departments & Programs
  • 4 Academic Divisions
  • 900+ Faculty Members
  • 6,000+ Undergraduate Students
  • 1,800+ Graduate Students

“The Faculty of Arts and Sciences is where Miskatonic's tradition of rigorous inquiry meets the urgent questions of our time. We prepare students not only for careers, but for lives of purpose and impact.”

— Octavian B. Kessler, Ph.D., Dean

Dean's Message

Dear Members of the Miskatonic Community,

Welcome to the Faculty of Arts and Sciences. As Dean, I am honored to lead a community of scholars and students who are passionately engaged in the pursuit of knowledge across the full spectrum of human inquiry.

Our faculty are leaders in their fields — from the humanities to the natural and social sciences. Our students are curious, driven, and committed to making a difference in the world. Together, we tackle the most pressing questions of our time, from climate change and public health to social justice and cultural preservation.

I invite you to explore our departments, our research, and our vibrant intellectual community. Whether you are a prospective student, a current member of our community, or an alum, you are an essential part of the FAS story.

— Octavian B. Kessler, Ph.D.
Dean, Faculty of Arts and Sciences

Research in the Faculty of Arts & Sciences

Our faculty and students pursue transformative research across the full spectrum of the humanities, social sciences, and natural sciences.

Humanities Research

Literary and linguistic analysis, historical studies, philosophical inquiry, and the preservation of cultural heritage through archival and digital methods.

Social Science Research

Empirical studies of human behavior, political systems, economic dynamics, social structures, and the institutions that shape society.

Natural Science Research

Discovery in physics, chemistry, biology, astronomy, and earth sciences — from the quantum scale to the cosmic.

Research Highlights

  • • Climate and Environmental Science: Interdisciplinary research on climate change, biodiversity loss, and sustainable solutions.
  • • Digital Humanities: Computational approaches to literary analysis, historical mapping, and cultural preservation.
  • • Social Inequality and Justice: Research on poverty, racism, gender equity, and the policies that address systemic injustice.
  • • Quantum and Materials Science: Fundamental research in quantum physics, materials chemistry, and photonics with applications in technology and medicine.

Culture & Arts

The Faculty of Arts and Sciences is home to a vibrant cultural life that enriches both the university and the wider community.

Miskatonic Conservatory

The Conservatory presents over 200 concerts annually, featuring student, faculty, and guest artists in classical, jazz, and contemporary music.

Miskatonic Museum

The museum houses extensive collections in natural history, archaeology, and cultural artifacts from around the world, with rotating exhibitions.

Arnaud’s Miskatonic Gallery

A Beaux-Arts gallery showcasing student and faculty creative works alongside masterpieces from the old masters and contemporary artists.

Bradley Abbot Performance Center

A 2,500-seat performing arts facility hosting theatre, dance, music, and lecture events throughout the academic year.

Community Engagement

The Faculty of Arts and Sciences is deeply committed to building bridges between the university and the wider community.

Public Lectures & Events

The FAS hosts a wide range of public events, including the Miskatonic Lecture Series, faculty panels, and community dialogues on pressing social issues.

K-12 Partnerships

Our faculty and students work with local schools through programs in science education, literacy, arts outreach, and college readiness.

Center for Service Learning

The Center connects students with community organizations, integrating service and civic engagement into the academic experience.

Arkham Cultural Exchange

A series of collaborative programs with Arkham's cultural institutions, including the Arkham Historical Society and the Arkham Public Library.

Leadership of the Faculty of Arts & Sciences

The FAS is guided by a dedicated team of academic leaders who shape the vision and direction of the faculty.
